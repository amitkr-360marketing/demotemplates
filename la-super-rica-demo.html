Some things can't be rushed.
Great tacos are one of them.

01

Masa, pressed to order

Every tortilla starts as fresh masa and hits the comal the moment you order. You can watch it happen through the kitchen window — that's the show.

02

The marinades, the rajas, the beans — made the same way since 1980. When something is right, you don't touch it.

Menu favorites

Order by number.
Remembered forever.

The full menu hangs above the window — these are the ones people drive hours for.

16

The Especial Most famous

Roasted pasilla chile stuffed with cheese, layered with marinated pork on fresh tortillas.

A

Adobado

Pork marinated overnight in red adobo, grilled and folded into pillowy handmade tortillas.

R

Rajas

Strips of roasted pasilla peppers with sautéed onions and melted cheese — the vegetarian legend.

C

Chorizo Especial

House chorizo with melted cheese, made for spooning onto tortillas straight off the comal.

F

Frijoles de la Casa

Slow-cooked beans with bacon, chile and spices — the side that outshines most mains.

H

Horchata & Aguas Frescas

Sweet, cold and made fresh — the only right answer to a warm Milpas afternoon.

Good to know

Questions, answered.

Is La Super-Rica really cash only?

Yes — cash only, and it's been that way for decades. There's an ATM within a short walk on Milpas Street.

What should I order on a first visit?

The #16 Especial is the dish that made us famous — roasted pasilla chile, melted cheese and marinated pork. Add the rajas if you want the vegetarian favorite, and horchata to cool it all down.

How long is the line?

It moves faster than it looks. Weekday afternoons and early evenings are calmest; weekend lunch is peak pilgrimage hour.

What did Julia Child order?

The legendary chef was a regular and brought friends whenever she could — she loved the handmade tortillas and called it her favorite spot for Mexican food in Santa Barbara.
